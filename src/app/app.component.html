RG Foundations

RG Foundations is an alternative educational setting built on the belief that every child deserves a learning environment where they are truly seen, supported, and empowered. Founded by former Special Education Teachers and current Board Certified Behavior Analysts, Christina Rodriguez and Alana Guilarte, RG Foundations was created in response to the gaps many students face in traditional classrooms. Our mission is to nurture confidence, independence, and a genuine love for learning through individualized instruction, compassionate guidance, and evidence-based strategies. We are committed to preparing each student for lifelong success, academically, socially, and emotionally.

At the heart of RG Foundations is the vision that every child who walks through our doors is capable of reaching their fullest potential when given the right tools, support, and opportunities. Christina and Alana’s combined experiences in both education and behavioral science shape a program that prioritizes meaningful progress, emotional safety, and holistic development. Through innovative teaching methods and a deeply personalized approach, we strive to create an inclusive, engaging, and empowering environment where students build essential skills and thrive beyond the classroom.

RG Foundations is more than a school, it is a community built on connection, collaboration, and shared purpose. We recognize the unique strengths and needs of each student and tailor instruction to honor their individuality. Through strong partnerships with families, structured support, and a warm, nurturing setting, we cultivate belonging, curiosity, and consistent growth. By celebrating progress at every stage and embedding values of confidence, independence, and resilience into each day, RG Foundations lays the foundation for a bright future and the lifelong success of every child we serve.

Educational Programming

At RG Foundations, we use the Unique Learning System for all students. The Unique Learning System is a comprehensive, standards-based curriculum designed primarily for students with disabilities, particularly those with cognitive impairments, autism, and other special educational needs. Its aim is to provide accessible, individualized, and engaging content to help students meet academic goals and develop essential life skills. Here's an overview of the key elements of the curriculum:

  • Differentiated Instruction: The curriculum is designed to meet the diverse needs of learners by offering materials and activities at varying levels of complexity. It allows educators to tailor lessons based on each student's abilities and learning pace.
  • Comprehensive Subject Areas: The system covers a broad range of subjects, including:
    • Math: Focuses on foundational skills such as counting, basic arithmetic, measurement, and problem-solving.
    • Language Arts: Includes reading comprehension, vocabulary development, writing, and communication skills.
    • Science and Social Studies: Engages students with simplified content related to scientific concepts and social studies topics like history, geography, and civics.
    • Social and Emotional Learning: Incorporates lessons that support emotional awareness, conflict resolution, and social skills development.
  • Assessment and Progress Monitoring: Unique Learning System includes tools for ongoing assessment of students' progress. Teachers can track individual performance and adjust instructional strategies accordingly.
  • Customization and Flexibility: The curriculum allows educators to modify lessons based on the specific needs and preferences of each student, ensuring that all learners can engage meaningfully with the content.

Handwriting

Handwriting Without Tears (HWT) is a comprehensive handwriting program designed to teach children how to print and write with ease, confidence, and legibility. Developed by Jan Olsen, a special education teacher, the program focuses on building a solid foundation in handwriting skills through a developmentally appropriate approach. It is often used in early childhood education settings, schools, and with students who have learning challenges.

Extracurriculars

At RG Foundations, students participate in a well-rounded, enriched school day that goes beyond academics. Our program includes thoughtfully designed Art, Music, and Physical Education experiences that support creativity, regulation, motor development, and social engagement. Through hands-on art projects, movement-based PE activities, and interactive music sessions, students build confidence while exploring new skills in a supportive and adaptable environment. Each activity is structured with individualized support to ensure every child can participate meaningfully, express themselves, and enjoy a balanced, engaging learning experience.
